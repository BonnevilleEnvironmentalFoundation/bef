Upcoming Webinar: Solar Electric Power at Stadiums and Arenas

Wednesday, September 18th
10:00am PST / 1:00pm EST

Provided by our partners at the Green Sports Alliance

.............................................................................................................................................

Across the sports industry, more and more professional teams and universities are installing solar arrays on or adjacent to sports facilities, and reducing their energy bills and carbon footprints in the process. The Bonneville Environmental Foundation (BEF), Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC) and the Alliance recently released an updated edition of their joint publication, Solar Electric Guide for Your Stadium or Arena: A guide to help professional and collegiate sports teams and venues develop successful on-site solar power generation. This updated guide includes case studies of successful solar installations from several Green Sports Alliance members and others.

On this webinar, we will hear from two Alliance members whose extensive solar installations are featured in the new guide, Arizona State University and Pocono Raceway, in addition to representatives from NRDC and BEF. Learn more about how solar power is energizing stadiums, arenas, and fans around the country, and how you can tap into this growing movement.

AGENDA

1. Allen Hershkowitz, Senior Scientist, NRDC and Board Member, Green Sports Alliance: Welcoming remarks and presenter introductions.

2. Val Fishman, VP Corporate Partnerships & Tiffany Meyer, Marketing Director, Bonneville Environmental Foundation: The growth of solar power across the sports industry, and highlights of their newly updated Solar Electric Guide for Your Stadium or Arena including how to effectively leverage your system as a community and fan engagement tool or to support sustainability goals.

3. Nick Brown, Director of University Sustainability Practices, Arizona State University: Solar installations on various ASU Athletics facilities, including atop Wells Fargo Arena, the home of Sun Devils Basketball.

4. George Ewald, Vice President and Track Superintendent, Pocono Raceway: How a brownfield adjacent to Pocono Raceway was transformed into a 3 megawatt solar farm that satisfies all the energy needs of the venue, and even feeds back into the grid.

5. Discussion/Q&A
